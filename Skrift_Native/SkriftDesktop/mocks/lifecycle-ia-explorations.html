Lifecycle IA — three ways to make every note tell one truth

The Unrated-shelf sketch (mocks/unrated-shelf.html) exposed a structural problem, not a copy bug: the Mac now has five overlapping state axes, and they can put two true-but-contradicting labels on one row — "the Mac is ignoring this" and "safe forever" at once. This doc explores three genuinely different information architectures that each leave every note with exactly one honest status. All three are derived-only: no store migration, no re-rating of existing notes, buildable against today's Memo + PipelineFile data.

#m0 · the problem, from source — five axes, one row, two truths
1

The process gate MemoCloudIngest.swift:39

guard processEverything || memo.significance > 0 — CloudKit syncs EVERY memo; this gate only decides whether one becomes a PipelineFile (a Queue row). An unrated memo is on the Mac's disk and in Review's river, but has no queue row, no enhancement, no export. The override is a buried Settings toggle, off by default (AppSettings.swift processAllSyncedMemos).

2

Importance Shared/Model/SignificanceScale.swift

10 circles (0.1–1.0), tiers Passing / Useful / Important at 0.4 / 0.7, refine wall at 0.8, 0 = Not rated. Fine as a control — the trouble is what else the value secretly does.

3

The fade lifecycle Shared/Pipeline/MemoLifecycle.swift

Untouched notes fade at 30d, auto-move to Recently Deleted at 60d, purge 14d later. But "touched" is ANY of nine signals — significance > 0, edits, title, tags, lock, reminder, annotation, keptAt, backlink. So significance is both the process gate AND just one of nine fade shields — "unrated" and "will fade" are different sets.

4

Queue processing state QueueDerivations.swift · AppModel.swift:5

Seven derived states (Queued → Transcribing → Transcribed → Enhancing → Ready → Exported / Error) plus the All / Needs Work / Done filter. Honest and unbroken — but it only exists for the rated minority that made it through axis 1.

5

Two "Recently Deleted"s RecentlyDeletedView.swift · FadingShelfColumn.swift (MacTrashColumn)

Same name, same 14-day countdown (DesktopTrash.swift:6 · Memo.swift:23), different stores: a Queue-footer sheet over PipelineFile (only ever rated notes) and a Review shelf over Memo (everything). One deletion of a synced note puts a row in BOTH; they re-converge only via two mirror hops (MacCloudDeleteSync.swift ↑ · MemoCloudUpdate.swift:39 ↓). A deleted unrated note exists only in the second.

the shared spine · one status, computed once — every direction runs on this

A single priority chain over Memo ⟕ PipelineFile (they already share an id by construction — MemoCloudIngest.swift:41). First match wins, so no note can ever carry two labels. the siding — ⏺ Parked: touched but never rated. Stays indefinitely, processed never. This is the zombie quadrant, finally named. One-liner: "kept — tagged" / "kept — locked", no countdown ever.

The one-home law: every station gets exactly ONE counting surface — one list whose number is that station's truth. Editorial views (the river, day lists, map, search) may show any live note, but no station is ever counted in two places, and no two surfaces may share a name over different stores. A countdown may only appear on stations that have one (Fading, Deleted — computed, so it can't lie).

New · Parked → direction-specific (the fight below) To process → In Obsidian → the Queue / desk Fading → its shelf Deleted → ONE Recently Deleted (Review)

Fine print: Mac-local uploads (drag-drop — PipelineFile with no Memo) live on the active track only and never fade. A phone note still transcribing is New (the river's slim row). A rated note whose queue row hasn't reconciled yet counts as To process. All derived — keptAt stays the only stored lifecycle bit.

The three directions below all compute THIS. They differ in what the user sees: one kills the rooms, one keeps the rooms and fixes their lies, one reorganizes around what happens next.

#m1 · direction 1 — ONE LEDGER NEW SURFACE

Core idea: there are no places, only filters — kill the Queue | Review split and every shelf; one chronological ledger of ALL notes with the spine status as a chip on each row; "Fading", "Deleted", "Ready" become scopes (saved filters with counts) over the same list, and the calendar and places become filters too.

Default scope "Notes" excludes Fading + Deleted (fade still empties the room — the locked behavior). "Needs me" = Ready + Stuck + fades-within-7-days: the one triage view. Every row: rate, Process, tag, delete in place. One row idiom everywhere — the river's cards and the queue's rows stop being two renderings of the same thing.

Axis 1 · gateStops being invisible: an unrated note is an ordinary row whose chip says why it isn't moving ("untouched — starts fading 19 Aug"). Process action on every row. Nothing is hidden, so nothing is "missing".
Axis 2 · importanceTen dots ON the row (drawn from JournalView.swift:486 importanceDots) — metadata, not a place. The 10-circle control stays in the note detail, unchanged.
Axis 3 · fadeThe Fading scope + honest chips from the spine. Sweep mechanics unchanged.
Axis 4 · queue stateThe pills survive as the chip for active-track rows; the All / Needs Work / Done chips die in favor of the "Needs me" and "In motion" scopes.
Axis 5 · trashesOne "Recently Deleted" scope over Memo, with Mac-only local uploads unioned in as a tail. The Queue footer sheet dies.
KillsThe Queue|Review switch, both shelf rows, the second trash, the row/card duality, and the dead-end click — a card IS the row.
CostsThe biggest rebuild of the three (a new shell), and the Lookback river — "a month ago, a year ago" — loses its home: it would shrink to a strip above the ledger. Connections, map mode, day view all re-anchor to scopes.
Honest failure modeSkrift becomes inventory software. The journal's designed calm — thinking that resurfaces on its own — inverts into you managing rows with chips. If the north is "notes that come back to you", the ledger is where that dies quietly, one status chip at a time.
#m2 · direction 2 — TWO ROOMS, ONE SPINE MOSTLY EXISTING — NEW PARTS RINGED

Core idea: keep the two rooms — the Queue is the machine's room, Review is yours — but bind every label in both to the one spine and give every station exactly one home. The Queue stops hiding notes: a collapsed "Not in the pipeline" band shows what the gate is skipping, right where you look for a missing note. The trash count drops to one. The rooms stay; the lies between them go.

#m3 · direction 3 — DEPARTURES NEW SURFACE — pills/cards from source

Status stops describing the past and starts describing the future — which is the only thing a lifecycle app actually owes you.

One list, one ordering, one verb. "Bring back" = Keep for a fading note, Restore for a deleted one — the user never needed two words for that. Purge dates keep the "~": your iPhone does the deleting (TrashPolicy 14d, Memo.swift:23).

Axis 2 · importanceThe steering wheel: rating moves a note between destinies; the value still tunes the refine wall (0.8) and export exactly as today. Control unchanged.
Axis 3 · fadeThe "→ out" band's forecasts + the conveyor. Fade / trash / purge = one line with visible position (the 3-dot mini-track).
Axis 4 · queue stateProgress within "→ Obsidian"; the pills survive as secondary detail ("processing now" / "ready").

RECOMMENDATION — Direction 2 as the chassis, adopting Direction 3's conveyor as its shelf model.

Why 2: the two rooms are load-bearing — Queue|Review encodes working-vs-remembering, signed in journal-desktop.html and lived in daily; Direction 1 buys "one truth" by killing the thing the app is actually for. And 2 attacks exactly the three broken axes (1, 3, 5) while leaving the two healthy ones (2, 4) alone.

Why borrow the conveyor: Direction 2 alone leaves the prettiest redundancy standing — Fading and Recently Deleted are one journey cut in half, with two verbs that both mean "bring back". The conveyor is separable and composes cleanly: it's a Review column swap, exactly like today's shelves. (If it feels like too much at once, ship 2 with the shelves as-is; the spine makes the merge a later drop-in.)

Build order (each commit-sized, all derived): ① the spine as a Shared type (status enum + one-liner strings, unit-tested — the copy matrix is a test table) → ② the queue band + read-only detail for unpipelined cards (kills the RootView:34 flash) → ③ retire the queue trash sheet → ④ merge the shelves into "On its way out" + move the sweep to a daily timer. Direction 3's destiny bands stay open as a later re-grouping of the same spine — nothing in ①–④ closes that door.
the decisions this doc needs from you
  • Q1 — May the Queue show unpipelined notes at all? The band is my pick because "where's my note" gets asked AT the queue. If you want the queue pipeline-pure, the fallback is the Unrated shelf (unrated-shelf.html) in Review — but then the mystery survives in the room where it's felt.
  • Q2 — Band "Process" = set 0.1? (Option A of unrated-shelf.html #m3, carried over.) Note this makes the Mac write Memo.significance up through CloudKit — same lane as Keep/Restore, but a new field for Mac-side writes. OK?
  • Q3 — The zombie's name. "Parked"? (alternatives: Held / Kept out). And does it deserve a counting surface anywhere, or is honest per-row copy enough? My pick: copy only — a place would re-create the shelf problem we're killing.
  • Q4 — Merge Fading + Recently Deleted into "On its way out" with one "Bring back" verb? If yes: the sweep moves off Review-refresh onto a daily timer (so shown dates are true), and the phone's ⋯ menu shows one item instead of two.
  • Q5 — Retiring the queue trash sheet (the locked one-trash decision): OK that Mac-only local uploads (no Memo) appear as a small "Mac-only files" tail in Review's list?
  • Q6 — processEverything's fate: keep the Settings toggle as an escape hatch, or retire it once the band's "Process all N" exists? My pick: retire — one visible control beats one buried one.
  • Q7 — The copy trio. Everything above uses three phrases for three events: "starts fading" (day 30, leaves the main surfaces) · "moves to Recently Deleted" (day 60) · "gone for good ~" (day 74, iPhone purges). Sign the trio off and every surface reuses it verbatim from the spine.
