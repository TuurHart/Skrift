Per-book audiobook sync

Books are local by default. You flip “Sync this book” on the one you’re reading → its position, bookmarks and audio sync to your other devices so you resume anywhere. Position + bookmarks are cheap and always sync once a book is on; the audio (large) syncs only for books you turn on.

Behaviour (the spec)

  • Default = local. The existing AudiobookLibraryStore / library.json is untouched; a book you don’t opt in never leaves the device. No risky whole-library migration.
  • Toggle = per book. Library long-press or player ⋯ → “Sync this book to my devices”. Turning it on creates a CloudKit-synced record for that book (id, title, author, chapters, duration, position, rate, bookmarks) and uploads its audio files as CKAssets (reuses the Phase-1c AssetMaterializer).
  • Why per-book (not all books): each device mints its own book id at import, so two independently-imported copies can never match for resume-sync — and syncing every entry would litter the other device with un-playable “phantom” books. Opting a book in gives it ONE shared id across devices.
  • Source device: shows “Uploading audio · N%”, then a teal “Synced” once done. A subtle “Syncing…” chip in the Library header while any upload/download is in flight.
  • Receiver device: the book appears with a download glyph → “Downloading to play here · N%”. Position + bookmarks are already there (cheap), so when audio lands it opens right where you left off. Wi-Fi → auto-download; cellular → don’t auto-pull but show a “Ready to sync · 240 MB” affordance to tap-to-download on data. Upload follows the same policy (no silent 240 MB push over cellular).
  • Two concepts (the Apple Books model — locked for v1): (1) the Sync toggle = is this book in your cross-device library? Off (from any device) unshares for all: removes the cloud entry + uploaded audio (frees iCloud) — but never deletes audio already on a device’s disk (it reverts to local-only there; a device that only had the cloud entry just loses the entry). (2) a per-device “Remove download” = free space on THIS device while the book stays synced (re-download later) — Books/Podcasts’ download-vs-library split.
  • Storage: the toggle shows the per-book size (“240 MB”); Settings gets a “Synced audiobooks” section — count + total (e.g. “3 books · 1.2 GB”) + per-book remove, and a gentle heads-up for a very large book (>1 GB). We show OUR usage only (CloudKit doesn’t expose the iCloud quota) + a “Manage in iCloud Settings” link — no “of X GB” claim.
  • BookTranscript (whole-book read-along text) stays device-local — its signature is the device’s own file size/mtime, so it re-derives per device.

Resolved — locked 2026-06-18

  • Toggle home: BOTH — library long-press menu + player ⋯ (same sheet from either).
  • Auto-download: Wi-Fi auto; cellular shows “ready to sync”, tap to download on data (same for upload).
  • Turn off: Apple Books model — global unshare keeps local copies; plus a per-device “Remove download” in v1.
  • Storage: per-book size on the toggle + a Settings “Synced audiobooks” section (count/total/per-book remove, >1 GB heads-up); no iCloud-quota claim.
