Build notes — this MIRRORS the desktop; it does not invent

• Same three operations as the Mac: copyEdit + title + summary (EnhancementService.swift), using the same prompt templates (AppSettings.Prompts — EN/NL preserved, no translation, fillers removed, no rewriting). No "Note / Bullets / Email / To-do" modes — that was an invention and is dropped.

• No raw/polished toggle. The Mac review shows the best body by precedence (sanitised → enhancedCopyedit → transcript) — there is no toggle, so the phone does the same: the polished body just is the body. The raw text is still the source of truth under the hood (and still what's sent to a paired Mac); "redo/re-transcribe" recovers it.

• ⋯ Redo menu = the Mac's NoteActions: Redo title / Redo copy-edit / Redo summary / Re-transcribe (copy-edit + re-transcribe hide for diarized conversations, as on the Mac).

• Title chooser = the Mac's NoteProperties two-card "Suggested (✦) vs From recording (〜)"; summary = the Mac's editable italic + accent-bar field. Both editable, autosave.

• Models tab extends today's read-only ModelInventory (Transcription / Speaker recognition / Custom-word spotting, with real labels + size-on-disk) into a managed view, and adds the Polish row.

Why the ranking, not "Qwen ≈ Apple"

Polish quality, best→worst: Mac Gemma E4B (paired) > Apple Intelligence (~3B, on the Neural Engine, free, zero added app-memory — but only 15 Pro+/M-series) > a small bundled model (1–2B, noticeably worse) > no-polish. The small model is a last resort for chips that can't run Apple Intelligence and aren't paired to a Mac — it is not "as good," so on a 15 Pro you'd never download one (Apple's is already there, free, better). Whether a bundled model ships at all (and which) is decided by the iPhone-13 spike; until then the iPhone-13 Polish row reads "Not available — use a Mac," per "better no model than an unreliable one."
