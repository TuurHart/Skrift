Decisions
Q1 — m1 (button + honest microcopy) or m2 (circles in the peek)? I'd take m2: it deletes the "strange system" (a score you never chose) instead of captioning it, reuses the one rating idiom, and a lazy flag is still a single click on circle 1.

Q2 — Where does Delete live? Proposal: everywhere the note is triaged — peek footer + quiet-row right-click + conveyor rows. Always soft (Recently Deleted, 14 days), so no confirm dialog on the Mac.

Q3 — Does the explanatory sentence replace "kept — edited" everywhere? Proposal: no — rows keep the terse spine one-liners; only the peek (where there's room and the confusion happened) expands to the sentence.

Q4 — Photos: render at the [[img_NNN]] marker position (as mocked), tap for Quick Look — same rule both apps. Multi-photo memos just render each marker.

Q5 — Adopt the one-clock model (m5)? It's the version of fading that actually keeps crap from piling up (Parked hoards forever today), and it shrinks the vocabulary to two words + two verbs. Cost: touched-but-never-rated notes eventually fade (~74 visible, restorable days). This revises the 2026-07-17 "touched never fades" doctrine — your call. If yes: "On its way out" → Fading everywhere, "kept — edited" chips → "fades 19 Sep", and m1/m2's peek sentence swaps to the clock wording.
