Sapiens
Yuval Noah Harari
Ch 4 · The Flood
Tolerance is not a Sapiens trademark. In modern times, a small difference in skin colour, dialect or religion has been enough to prompt one group to set about exterminating another.
Would ancient humans have been more tolerant towards an entirely different human species? It may well be that when Sapiens encountered Neanderthals, the result was the first and most significant ethnic-cleansing campaign in history.
On the other hand, the two species may have found each other sexually attractive, and the differences between them dissolved into a single interbreeding population.
Whatever the case, the Neanderthals (and the other human species) pose one of history's great what-ifs. Imagine how things might have turned out had the Neanderthals or Denisovans survived alongside Homo sapiens.

What changes

  • Tap-to-fold, tap-to-unfold. The bookmark lives on the page — tap the gutter left of a passage and the purple bookmark drops in beside it (+ a faint tint). Tap it again and it lifts off. (Before: the glyph wasn't tappable — you couldn't remove it.)
  • No bottom "Mark" button. The dedicated toggle in the control row is gone — folding is the gesture. (Before: Mark sat in the bottom row, detached from the text.)
  • Contents sheet jumps. One sheet, two tabs — Chapters to skip chapters, Bookmarks to jump straight to a fold. Tapping a bookmark scrolls to it.

Try it: tap a margin to fold, tap again to unfold, then open Contents → Bookmarks to see the list update live.
