Books tab + one-tap resume

Kills the Highlights placeholder, renames Library → Books, and makes the listening session follow you: the capsule is global, the last book restores paused at launch, and tapping a book plays it.

Thesis: both "first things" — record a memo, continue the book — are one tap from the same launch screen. No guessing which mood you're in; a fixed home with both affordances.

v2 — rebuilt 1:1 against the app's real tokens & components (Theme.swift, MemoCard, AudiobookMiniPlayerBar, AudiobookLibraryView)
Behaviour rules this mock encodes
  • Highlights tab cut. Captures already live in Notes (book glyph + ❝-quote rows); a per-book notes surface comes later, in the book context, not as a tab.
  • Cold launch: the most recently played book restores as a paused capsule — never auto-plays, skipped if its audio isn't on this device. Play = 1 tap; record = 1 tap (FAB).
  • The capsule is global: every tab, and it persists over pushed screens (memo detail). Full-screen covers (recorder, player, capture) still hide it.
  • Tap a book row → it plays and the full player opens already running (the audiobook-app convention; open-paused was a dead extra tap).
  • PLAYING/PAUSED row badge dropped — the capsule + the row tint carry that state now.
  • The header chip is the sort/filter control: sort Recently played (default, persisted) · Title · Author · Recently added; status filters In progress · Not started · Finished (transient).
  • DECIDED 2026-07-07: screen title renamed "Notes" (matches the tab); "memo" → "note" across all user-facing copy.
  • DECIDED 2026-07-07: the verb is "Add note" everywhere — capsule pill, player chip, and the capture screen header.
