Naming review — opt-out model (signed off 2026-06-16)

Supersedes opt-in-naming.html. Names are recognised against the user's known roster and the note's subjects are linked by default (opt-out); you prune the side-mentions. The whole interaction lives in the prose — three tiers + a click-popover. No chip bar, no pending bar. Deterministic + LLM-free (phone-portable). Full rationale + build-guards: NAMING_MODEL.md.

“Unlink” is the everyday opt-out gesture (demote a side-mention). The unlinked name stays a dotted suggestion — re-promotable. “Change person” fixes a wrong match.
The model, in one breath
  • ✅ Job: one note per person; subjects collect as dated backlinks → a decades-long timeline.
  • ✅ Opt-out: known people auto-link (first mention); you prune side-mentions.
  • ✅ Risk-tiered: full/distinctive names auto-commit; common-word + ambiguous names → suggested.
  • ✅ Roster = Skrift’s portable names DB (seeded from People/ on Mac; canonical = note title).
  • ✅ New people: manual, one-keystroke add. No NER, no LLM. “Never misses KNOWN people.”
  • ✅ Output: one body link (the snippet) + people: frontmatter (durable + queryable).
