Build notes — proposed decisions (2026-06-25)

  • What syncs: the Mac writes a MemoEnhancement sidecar (copy-edit · title · summary + provenance) to the shared CloudKit Memo store; it lands on the phone. The phone re-links names over the copy-edit (shared Sanitiser) — same deterministic engine as the export, no on-phone LLM.
  • Body = Polished / Original toggle. Default Polished when a MemoEnhancement.hasContent exists (best-body precedence, matching the export). Original = the editable RAW transcript (the task-1 name-linking surface). No toggle shows at all when there's no polish — just the raw editor as today.
  • Polished is READ-only; Original is editable. Clean separation: the polish is the Mac's derived result you read; edits happen on the RAW (the contract source of truth). Avoids the "who owns the edit" ambiguity.
  • Title chooser (the parked phone problem) = a compact bottom sheet, not two cards. The ✦ button opens a list: Suggested (the Mac's LLM title, ✦) · From the recording (transcript first line) · your own (edit). A check marks the active one. Picking the Mac title just adopts it as memo.title; "your own" opens the inline title field. Replaces the desktop's cramped Suggested/From-recording two-card chooser.
  • Summary card (✦) above the body when the Mac wrote one; hidden otherwise.
  • Provenance: a quiet "✦ Polished on your Mac · <ago>" caption so it's clear the polish came from the paired Mac (not invented on-device).
  • Name tiers still apply (task 1): linked names render accent in BOTH bodies; tap-to-resolve works on the editable Original. (Open Q for sign-off: allow tap-resolve on the read-only Polished too, or only Original?)
  • No on-device polish here — that's the separate Phase-4 spike (Apple-Intelligence / bundled model). This screen only surfaces what a paired Mac produced.
